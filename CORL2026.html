About

Robot manipulation is moving beyond simple pick-and-place tasks. More capable embodiments, end-effectors, sensors, learning algorithms, and large-scale data now make it possible to study harder tasks: cutting fruit, cracking eggs, using tools, handling delicate materials, and interacting with articulated objects.

These settings need world models that predict more than appearance. A physical world model should represent action-relevant physical state: how objects move under contact, which actions are feasible, how force, compliance, articulation, material, temperature, and uncertainty change the outcome, and how these quantities can be inferred from RGB-D, tactile, force, thermal, audio, and proprioceptive data.

PhysicalWM asks a focused question: what action-relevant physical state beyond visual inputs should a world model represent for robot manipulation, and how should that capability be evaluated?

Core Challenges

  1. Physical-state representation. Which decision-relevant quantities beyond RGB-D, such as contact, force, compliance, articulation, and uncertainty, should be represented explicitly or implicitly?
  2. State inference and prediction. How can physical states and action-conditioned transitions be learned from partially observed multimodal interaction data when direct supervision is limited?
  3. Manipulation-oriented evaluation. How should models be evaluated on prediction accuracy, physical consistency, uncertainty, and downstream utility for planning and control?
Workshop outcome

Participants will work toward a community-authored Physical World Model State and Evaluation Protocol for physically grounded robot manipulation.

Topics of Interest

  • Physical-state representations for action-conditioned world models
  • Contact-rich manipulation, tool use, deformable objects, dexterity, and humanoids
  • Learning force, compliance, articulation, friction, temperature, stability, and uncertainty
  • Multimodal sensing with RGB-D, tactile, force/torque, thermal, audio, and proprioception
  • State inference and prediction from robot logs, demonstrations, videos, and simulation
  • 3D and 4D scene representations, object-centric models, and physically grounded reconstruction
  • Simulation, differentiable physics, digital twins, real-to-sim, and sim-to-real transfer
  • Benchmarks, datasets, and evaluation protocols for manipulation-oriented world models

Call for Submissions

We will solicit non-archival submissions through the workshop website, mailing lists, research communities, and social media. Reviews will consider novelty, potential impact, and technical quality. Three submissions from each track will be selected for short oral presentations.

Poster track

Short papers, position papers, work-in-progress papers, negative results, benchmark or dataset proposals, and recent research results.

Demo track

Physical robot demos, executable world-model prototypes, multimodal sensing platforms, manipulation benchmarks, and interactive visualization tools.
